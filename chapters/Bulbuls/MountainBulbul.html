Mountain Bulbul(Ixos mcclellandii)
The Mountain Bulbul is a very handsome bulbul with its spiky crown, hence we have given it the affectionate nick-name “spiky head bulbul”. It is very common in the highlands and also a common participant of bird waves (mixed foraging parties). It has bright olive wings and a breast texture which gives a “blur” appearance. As most bulbuls it has a wide diet, usually berries and fruit. Bulbuls are often seen as pair (mates).

Family:Pycnonotidae
Scientific Name:Ixos mcclellandii
Local Name:Merbah Gunung
Status:Resident, common in the highlands
Size:About 22-24 cm (8-9 inches)
Key Features:Bright olive wings with a streaked breast and a hackled crown. Both sexes look alike.
Call:Calls are shrill and repetition of ‘chiep’ or ‘cheu’.
Food: Omnivorous – many sorts of insects, berries and fruits.
Nesting:A small nest cup a bush. Breeding usually March to October.
