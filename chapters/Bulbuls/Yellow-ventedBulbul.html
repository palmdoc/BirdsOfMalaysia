Yellow Vented Bulbul (Pycnonotus goiavier)
Yellow Vented Bulbuls are a very common garden bird in Peninsular Malaysia. They are predominantly brown birds, of medium size and easily recognised by their very pleasant, bubbly call – very much like their names “bulbul”. There is a patch of yellow at the vent (underside of body, just where the tail starts) which gives this bulbul their name. Both sexes look alike but you can differentiate them by their behaviour – the male plays the more protective role and sings much more than the female. They have a warm personality and know how to live for the moment. They make up part of the “dawn chorus” that we hear around our homes, singing with the rising sun – almost as though to welcome a new day, singing “good morning, good morning”.

Family:Pycnonotidae
Scientific Name:Pycnonotus goiavier
Local Name:Merbah Kapur
Status:Resident, very common & found in almost all city/town gardens
Size:About 20 cm (7.5 inches)
Key Features:Brown on the upper parts. White on underparts with some brown. Yellow under tail coverts, black strip on forehead. Black bill and feet. They have a small, dark brown crown that can be erected, especially if upset. Both sexes look alike.
Call: The common call is a bubbly, liquid string of notes. Occasionally there is a sharper note (“cheap”) of danger or warning.
Food:Omnivorous – many sorts of insects, berries and fruits. Have a preference for berries from Straits Rhododendron (Melastoma malabathricum), Ehretia (a common garden hedge in the past) and the fruit of the Neem tree. Also very fond of nectar from the Bottle Brush tree (Callistemon).
Nesting:Usually a small nest cup of straw, 0.3-2 meters (1-6 feet) off the ground in a small bush. May be higher. Usually two eggs laid. Breeding almost all year round, especially January to July.
